Dr. Sigmund Freud

The man who is the founder of psychoanalysis

Here's a time line of Dr. Sigmund Freud's life

  • 1856 (May 6) - Born in Freiberg, Moravia (now Příbor, Czech Republic).
  • 1873 - 1881 - Studied medicine at the University of Vienna.
  • 1881 - Received his medical degree.
  • 1885 - Studied under Jean-Martin Charcot in Paris, where he developed an interest in hysteria and neurology.
  • 1886 - Opened a private practice in neurology in Vienna.
  • 1889 - Birth of his first daughter, Mathilde.
  • 1893 - Birth of his second daughter, Anna Freud.
  • 1895 - Published "Studies on Hysteria" with Josef Breuer.
  • 1899 - Published "The Interpretation of Dreams," a foundational work in psychoanalysis.
  • 1902 - Freud appointed professor at the University of Vienna.
  • 1909 - Founded the International Psychoanalytical Association.
  • 1913 - Published "Totem and Taboo," exploring the origins of civilization.
  • 1917 - Freud experienced health issues and underwent surgery for oral cancer.
  • 1920 - Published "Beyond the Pleasure Principle."
  • 1923 - Published "The Ego and the Id," introducing the structural model of the mind.
  • 1927 - Freud visited the United States, meeting with prominent figures in psychology and delivering lectures at Clark University.
  • 1930 - Published "Civilization and Its Discontents."
  • 1933 - Nazi regime rises to power in Germany; Freud's books are among those burned by the Nazis.
  • 1938 - Freud and his family fled Austria to escape the Nazi persecution and settled in London.
  • 1939 - Freud died on September 23 in London.
